About This Project

This site accompanies my work in the Gaming Seminar SS2026. Over the course of the semester, I am developing a serious game that makes algorithmic bias tangible through play — turning the invisible mechanics of automated decision-making into something you can experience firsthand.

The Game
Hiring Algorithm
A Twine-based narrative game about AI bias in hiring

Step into the role of an HR algorithm. Review applications, make decisions, and discover how gender and age bias creep into automated systems — not through malice, but through mechanics.

Twine Serious Game AI Ethics Procedural Rhetoric
In development — launching Summer 2026
Theoretical Background

The game draws on Bogost’s concept of procedural rhetoric — the idea that a game’s rules are its argument. The bias isn’t explained through text; it emerges from the system the player operates within. Grounded in research on word embedding bias (Bolukbasi et al., 2016) and the notion that prototypes themselves constitute theory (Galey & Ruecker, 2010).
