Intervals in the Dark Forest

Wednesday evening, just before full dark. The kind of run where the headlamp barely cuts through the fog and the trail exists mostly as a memory. Fast bursts, slow recovery, repeat. No pace display, no other runners — just effort and rest and the sound of branches cracking somewhere off the path.

There's something about running intervals with almost no visibility that strips away all the noise. The body figures it out on its own.
